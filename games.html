Count Meowcula's Barbershop

Aug. 2025

You're Count Meowcula, a vampire cat that opened a barbershop. Wash and cut hairs, clean your establishment, manage your instincts and try not to bite your clients.

My first game on Steam! Also part of Lumo Entertainment's Lumo LAB project. I stood more on the coding side of things here, notably setting up the game's Steamworks page and integration within the game, as well as Discord Rich Presence!

Learning how to use Steamworks, setting up achievements, stats, Steam Cloud etc, was tough in the beginning but, not gonna lie, it was fun. You should give it a try!

Snake.exit

Apr. 2025

Escape from your own app, traveling through other applications and evolving from an old Nokia to the most recent smartphones.

This game was made for the game jam that happened during Lumo LAB's bootcamp, a project from Lumo Entertainment. This was my first time in a 3-week game jam, and my first time in a game jam in a long time overall. This was also where I acted as the main sound designer in a game for the first time! I decided to step in for this task since no one in our group had any idea how to make music or SFX besides me.

I admit there are some things that could use some better polishment, but I'm honestly impressed by how much we could pull out in such a short time!

(The game is currently in Portuguese only)

Alnarya

Dec. 2024

You are Eladan, an young elf chosen by Alnarya, the Tree of Dreams, to defeat Kharon and save the world from his curse.

My first game made as a project for college. Our assignment was to make a mobile platform game, so that's what we did. Despite all the complications our team had during development, I think it came out pretty well.

We plan on making a finished version of this game eventually, with better visuals, an intro, a final boss etc. Nothing too complicated, though.

(The game is currently in Portuguese only)

Lost, INC.

Jan. 2023

You got the job. Don't mess this up.

Lost, INC. is a game where you work for a lost and found company. Find items reported missing around the city and return them to their respective owners' doors. Do make sure to do your job well, or else your boss will not be happy.

Made for the 44th gm48, theme "Delivery". Used the game jam's leaderboards system for displaying best scores among players. This was also the project I was in charge for most stuff. Also everything almost blew up towards the end!

Spread a Smile

Apr. 2022

Collect candy around the streets, then head to some house to shoot "baddies" out of people's heads. Try getting the best score and number of candies in the world!

My team's best-placing game in gm48 to this day. The jam's theme was "Non-Violence", so we tried to come up with something that's borderline "violent", but not quite there.

People really liked the concept of the game, and felt very competitive fighting for the best score.

Dantoma

Oct. 2021

The big evil ghost stole your potion recipes book! Explore a haunted library, fight ghosts with your three magic spells and take back what's yours!

Of all my jam games, Dantoma is my personal favorite. This was the first time I actually worked with a team on a game AND made a procedural generation system for the level (albeit a simple one). Made for the 40th gm48, theme "Discovery".

We liked this game so much, we're actually working on a full version of it! It's going slow, but it's going!

Burning Down

Oct. 2021

The house is on fire! Get inside and save all people, using your extinguisher to put down fire and access other areas!

Burning Down was my first jam game ever. Other than the sound effects, everything was made all by myself. Made for LD49, theme "Unstable".

I joined this jam mostly for forcing myself into Unity. I'm glad I managed to put all of this together in under 72 hours. The game was praised for having that much content made by a first timer in such a short time!
